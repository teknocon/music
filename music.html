The Artist

A Life Devoted to
the Raga

Born into a family of musicians in Varanasi, Pandit Arjun Mehta began his training at the age of five under the tutelage of his father, the late Pt. Raghunath Mehta, a noted sitarist of the Benares gharana.

At sixteen, he was accepted as a disciple by the legendary Ustad Ali Akbar Khan, through whom he imbibed the profound aesthetic sensibilities of the Maihar Gharana — a tradition that values emotional depth, technical precision, and spiritual surrender in equal measure.

Over four decades, Pandit Mehta has performed at the most prestigious venues across the world — from the Sawai Gandharva Bhimsen Festival to Carnegie Hall — earning recognition as one of the foremost sitar exponents of his generation.

The Melodic Universe

Featured Ragas

Each raga is a living entity — with its own personality, its own time of day, its own emotional landscape. Here are some of the ragas closest to my heart.

Evening

Raga Yaman

Kalyan Thaat · Sampurna

The queen of evening ragas — majestic, serene, and infinitely deep. Its soaring Gandhar and gentle Nishad evoke the golden hour when day surrenders to dusk.

Late Night

Raga Darbari Kanada

Asavari Thaat · Sampurna

The raga of the royal court — weighty, ponderous, and devastatingly beautiful. Its heavy oscillations (andolan) on Gandhar and Dhaivat create an atmosphere of grand introspection.

Spring / Morning

Raga Basant

Purvi Thaat · Audav-Sampurna

The fragrance of spring captured in sound — vivacious yet devotional. Shuddha and Tivra Madhyam dance together, mirroring the playfulness of new blossoms in a mustard field.

Swara set
S R G m M P N
Early Morning

Raga Bhairavi

Bhairavi Thaat · Sampurna

The mother of all ragas — tender, devotional, and all-encompassing. Bhairavi embraces every note with the warmth of a mother's love, making it the perfect vehicle for thumri and bhajan.

Monsoon

Raga Megh Malhar

Kafi Thaat · Audav

The rain-bringer — Tansen's legendary raga that could summon clouds. Its sweeping phrases and Komal Nishad evoke the vast, dark monsoon sky over the Indian subcontinent.

Midnight

Raga Malkauns

Bhairavi Thaat · Audav

Ancient, pentatonic, and profoundly meditative. Five notes, infinite depth. Malkauns strips music to its essence — pure melody unadorned, calling forth the stillness of midnight.

Guru-Shishya Parampara

The Lineage

Knowledge flows from master to disciple — an unbroken chain of musical wisdom spanning over a century.

1862 — 1972

Ustad Allauddin Khan

The legendary "Baba" of Maihar — founder of the Maihar Gharana, multi-instrumentalist, and guru to some of India's greatest musicians including Ravi Shankar and Ali Akbar Khan.

1922 — 2009

Ustad Ali Akbar Khan

The "Emperor of Sarod" — Pandit Mehta's primary guru, who shaped his aesthetic vision and taught him that music is not about notes but about the spaces between them.

1938 — 2010

Pt. Raghunath Mehta

Father and first guru — a sitarist of the Benares Gharana who instilled the foundations of raga grammar and the discipline of daily practice (riyaz) from the age of five.

b. 1968

Pt. Arjun Mehta

Carrying the flame forward — performing, teaching, and exploring new frontiers while remaining rooted in the timeless tradition of Hindustani classical music.
